# GraphReFly Stack — expanded production prompt

## Style block

Use the exact review-product identity from `design.md`: `#dfe9ef` canvas, `#173044` ink,
`#f7fafb` paper, `#246482` Blueprint, `#39765f` valid, `#963e68` blocked, `#b6c8d1` rules, and
`#f0a45d` focus. `Avenir Next` is the human intent voice and `SFMono-Regular` is the evidence voice.
Square geometry, editorial architecture-review mood, no generic neon developer styling.

## Rhythm

`hook — tension — build — PEAK — proof — recover — resolve`

Seven scenes, 165 seconds. Primary transition is a 0.5-second medium blur crossfade. The semantic
invalidation peak uses one 0.35-second blocked-color dip. Final resolution fades gently to the canvas.
Every scene has a build, readable hold, and one seekable ambient motion.

## Global production rules

- 1920 × 1080 at 30 fps, English voiceover, under three minutes.
- Use real captured review UI for the product proof. Never use an iframe.
- Two focal points per scene: narrative statement plus evidence/UI.
- Background depth uses a 48px architecture grid, one oversized ghost commit or reason code, two
  structural rules, and a localized Blueprint or status field.
- Captions are precise four-to-six-word groups on a paper rail, with Blueprint emphasis and hard
  deterministic kills.
- Scene entrances vary draw, cascade, slide, stamp, and focus motions with at least three eases.
- No per-scene color invention. No model-confidence visualization. No approval or merge animation.

## Scene 1 — Git's blind spot, 0–18s

**Concept:** A clean Git rebase lands with institutional certainty, then the word “valid?” interrupts
it. The viewer should feel the gap between textual success and architectural truth.

**Depth:** BG grid, drifting `REBASE 0 CONFLICTS`, Blueprint focus field. MG large hook and two
contrasting verdict stamps. FG timecode, hairline, product lockup, commit metadata.

**Choreography:** Hook DRAWS from the left; clean-rebase stamp STAMPS valid green; “architecture
valid?” SLIDES from the opposite edge and lands blocked; rules grow outward; commit digits drift.

**Transition:** Blur crossfade, 0.5s.

## Scene 2 — GPT-5.6 plans a real stack, 18–42s

**Concept:** Three work units assemble as a dependency-aware real stack. GPT-5.6 provides semantic
language while locked anchors visibly constrain it.

**Depth:** BG task hash and prompt-version ghost type. MG U1/U2/U3 stack cards and dependency line.
FG `gpt-5.6-sol · high`, strict-schema badge, source-scope labels, one warm focus marker.

**Choreography:** U1, U2, U3 CASCADE with distinct directions; dependency line DRAWS; model chip
FOCUSES; locked anchors STAMP in monospace; a Blueprint aura breathes once.

**Transition:** Blur crossfade, 0.5s.

## Scene 3 — High-level semantic review, 42–65s

**Concept:** The actual product fills the frame in a perspective-treated capture. A reviewer selects
real commits and sees intent, structural delta, claim impact, checks, and gate before raw code.

**Depth:** BG canvas/grid. MG captured UI in a raised device-like frame. FG three numbered callouts,
selection rail, and `REAL GIT · READ ONLY` metadata.

**Choreography:** UI frame FOCUSES from slight perspective; callouts CASCADE; selection line DRAWS;
commit ID types on; capture slowly pans by less than three percent.

**Transition:** Blur crossfade, 0.5s.

## Scene 4 — Clean rebase, stale architecture, 65–92s

**Concept:** The architecture introduces `sessionMutationBroker` without touching feature files. A
zero-conflict rebase is visually separated from the changed Blueprint witness.

**Depth:** BG two disjoint file columns and ghost `0 CONFLICTS`. MG captured U2 review plus a focused
broker node. FG `BLUEPRINT_CHANGED` and `POLICY_CHANGED` witnesses, exact policy versions, arrows.

**Choreography:** File columns SLIDE apart; zero-conflict marker STAMPS; broker node DROPS between
intent and persistence; reason codes PUNCH in; current witness line traces across the frame.

**Transition:** Quick blocked-color dip, 0.35s.

## Scene 5 — Deterministic selective invalidation, 92–118s

**Concept:** The gate is the peak: U1 remains green, U2 is blocked for exact witnesses, U3 is blocked
only through dependency. No confidence meter appears.

**Depth:** BG canonical input digest and gate-version type. MG three-unit verdict rail and real U2/UI
capture. FG exact ordered reasons, check dots, `GateResult · read only`, and a claim/evidence/verdict
separator.

**Choreography:** U1 FILLS green; U2 and U3 verdicts STAMP blocked at different weights; reason codes
CASCADE in canonical order; dependency line pulses once; read-only rule DRAWS and holds.

**Transition:** Blur crossfade, 0.5s.

## Scene 6 — Selective replan and recovery, 118–145s

**Concept:** GPT-5.6 replans only U2 and U3. Fresh witnesses replace stale bindings, U1 is visibly
preserved, and the final deterministic gate returns pass.

**Depth:** BG response digest and token-usage columns. MG preserved U1 plus rebinding U2/U3 rails.
FG model/runtime provenance, `session-writes.v2`, final checks, and valid stamp.

**Choreography:** U1 HOLDS still; old U2/U3 witnesses de-focus through the transition; new witnesses
REBind with horizontal motion; check dots COUNT on; final PASS STAMPS with a restrained overshoot.

**Transition:** Gentle blur crossfade, 0.6s.

## Scene 7 — Evidence, boundary, impact, 145–165s

**Concept:** Close on review compression, not blind trust. Claim, evidence, and verdict remain three
separate objects, bound into a portable redacted bundle.

**Depth:** BG `24 HASHED ARTIFACTS` ghost type and grid. MG three-part trust flow plus product lockup.
FG exact model/effort, bundle icon, no-key judge path, and final tagline.

**Choreography:** Trust-flow objects DRAW in order; bundle pages CASCADE; `24` COUNTS quickly; tagline
FOCUSES; final rule expands then the scene gently fades to canvas.

## Recurring motifs

- A single Blueprint line travels through all scenes and changes from `#246482` to valid or blocked
  only when the deterministic gate does.
- Monospace commit fragments and digests occupy the frame edges as evidence, never decoration without
  meaning.
- Warm `#f0a45d` appears only on human focus and selection, never on verdicts.

## Negative prompt

No black terminal aesthetic, cyan/purple gradients, glowing AI brain, fake code rain, floating rounded
cards, confidence percentages, auto-merge, invented metrics, noncanonical reason codes, or claims of
deployment/publication. Do not obscure the real UI capture with captions or decoration.
